Boston Housing Dataset: CRIM Post-Outlier Removal

CRIM Column Analysis: Post-Outlier Removal

After removing outliers from the CRIM column (crime rate per capita by town), the distribution has become more consistent, highlighting typical trends in the data. This cleansing process makes it easier to spot meaningful patterns in crime rates across different neighborhoods, without the skew caused by extreme outliers.

Key Insights Post-Outlier Removal

  • Crime Rate Distribution: Post-outlier removal, the distribution of the crime rate per capita has normalized, reducing the influence of exceptionally high crime values that previously dominated the analysis. This provides a clearer picture of crime across the Boston housing dataset.
  • Urban Areas: After removing extreme outliers, the dataset now better reflects crime rates in typical urban and suburban areas, helping to distinguish between low-crime and high-crime neighborhoods more effectively.

Improved Visual Insights

  • CRIM vs Other Variables: With outliers removed, the scatter plots between crime rate and other variables (e.g., housing prices) reveal more focused trends, allowing for a better understanding of how crime correlates with factors like median home value.
  • CRIM Distribution: The histogram of crime rates now appears more symmetric, showing a clearer range of crime values across neighborhoods, without the extreme influence of outlier values.

Removing the outliers from the CRIM column has improved both the quality of visualizations and the clarity of insights. The dataset now offers a more accurate reflection of typical crime rates, paving the way for better predictive modeling and analysis.
