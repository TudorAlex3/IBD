Data Analysis Summary

This analysis focuses on the **Human Activity Recognition (HAR)** dataset, which consists of time-series data collected from accelerometers and gyroscopes. Scatter plots visualize the relationships between key features, helping to detect trends, anomalies, and outliers. These visualizations aid in understanding the distinct patterns across activities and sensors.

Feature Description Key Observations Outliers (Count)
tBodyAcc-mean()-X Average body acceleration along the X-axis. Derived from smartphone accelerometer data. Values near zero for stationary activities. Outliers observed for dynamic activities. 15
tBodyGyro-std()-Z Standard deviation of gyroscopic signals along the Z-axis. Reflects rotation variability. High variability during activities like walking upstairs. Minimal variation in stationary states. 10

Outlier Details

Notable observations based on the scatter plots:

  • tBodyAcc-mean()-X: Outliers include extremely high or low values during dynamic activities, likely caused by rapid directional changes or sensor noise.
  • tBodyGyro-std()-Z: Outliers appear during activities with frequent rotational movements, such as walking or jogging, indicating significant signal variance.
  • Feature Correlations: Strong correlations between certain features (e.g., accelerometer and gyroscope signals) are visible. Deviations may represent anomalies or unique cases.
