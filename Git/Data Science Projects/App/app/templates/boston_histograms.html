Boston Housing Dataset Description

Dataset Information

The Boston Housing dataset contains information on various features of houses in Boston, such as crime rates, property tax rates, and housing prices. It is commonly used for regression tasks, where the goal is to predict the median value of owner-occupied homes based on other features.

Column Details

  • CRIM: Crime rate by town (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • ZN: Proportion of residential land zoned for large lots (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • INDUS: Proportion of non-retail business acres per town (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • CHAS: Charles River dummy variable (1 if tract bounds river; 0 otherwise) (Categorical, 2 unique values).
  • NOX: Nitrogen oxides concentration (parts per 10 million) (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • RM: Average number of rooms per dwelling (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • AGE: Proportion of owner-occupied units built before 1940 (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • DIS: Weighted distance to employment centers (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • RAD: Index of accessibility to radial highways (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • TAX: Property tax rate per $10,000 (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • PTRATIO: Pupil-teacher ratio by town (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • B: Proportion of residents of African American descent (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • LSTAT: Percentage of lower status population (Numerical, 506 unique values).
  • MEDV: Median value of owner-occupied homes in $1000s (Target variable, Numerical, 506 unique values).

Handling Missing Values

Missing values in the Boston Housing dataset are minimal, with no significant missing data in the primary features. However, in some cases, rows might be removed or interpolated depending on the analysis approach.

Key Insights

The Boston Housing dataset is commonly used for regression tasks to predict housing prices based on the provided features. Some key insights from the dataset include:

  • Higher crime rates (CRIM) tend to correlate with lower property values (MEDV).
  • A higher number of rooms per dwelling (RM) generally correlates with a higher housing price (MEDV).
  • Proximity to the Charles River (CHAS) may have a slight positive effect on housing prices.
  • Higher property tax rates (TAX) negatively affect housing prices.
