Explanation

The confusion matrices (commented out above) summarize the performance of Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, and Random Forest models:

  • Diagonal entries represent correctly classified samples for different wine quality levels (e.g., 5, 6, 7).
  • Off-diagonal entries indicate misclassifications, where one class is predicted as another.
  • Classes with 0 Precision, Recall, and F1-score (such as 0, 4, 6, 9, 10, etc.) indicate that these classes were either not predicted or were poorly predicted by the model.
  • Classes with low support (e.g., 9, 10, 14) have few or no occurrences in the dataset, leading to unreliable predictions.
  • Random Forest performs best overall, achieving high precision and recall across several classes.
  • Logistic Regression and Decision Tree also perform well but have slightly higher misclassification rates compared to Random Forest.

Conclusion: Random Forest is the most accurate and reliable model for this dataset, exhibiting minimal misclassification. Logistic Regression and Decision Tree also perform effectively but have some limitations in predicting rare or underrepresented classes.
