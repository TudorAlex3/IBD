Dataset Overview

The Human Activity Recognition (HAR) dataset includes time-series data collected from accelerometers and gyroscopes embedded in smartphones. It contains features derived from body acceleration, gravity acceleration, and jerk signals for six human activities.

Explanation

The dataset consists of 561 features extracted from raw signals. Key columns include:

  • tBodyAcc-mean()-X, tBodyAcc-mean()-Y, tBodyAcc-mean()-Z: Mean values of body acceleration signals along X, Y, and Z axes.
  • tBodyAcc-std()-X, tBodyAcc-std()-Y, tBodyAcc-std()-Z: Standard deviation of body acceleration signals along X, Y, and Z axes.
  • tBodyGyro-mean()-X, tBodyGyro-mean()-Y, tBodyGyro-mean()-Z: Mean values of gyroscope signals along X, Y, and Z axes.
  • tBodyAccMag-std(): Standard deviation of the magnitude of body acceleration signals.
  • activity: The activity being performed (e.g., Walking, Sitting).
  • subject_id: Identifier for the individual performing the activity.

Activities Included

  • WALKING
  • WALKING_UPSTAIRS
  • WALKING_DOWNSTAIRS
  • SITTING
  • STANDING
  • LAYING
