Boston Housing Dataset: CRIM Outliers

CRIM Column Analysis: Outliers

The CRIM column represents the crime rate per capita by town. Higher values indicate areas with higher crime rates, and lower values suggest safer neighborhoods. Outliers in the CRIM column are valuable for identifying areas with abnormally high crime rates, which may reflect extreme cases such as urban centers with significant crime problems.

Metric Description Outliers (Count) Key Observations
CRIM Crime rate in the area (per capita) 12 Outliers represent areas with significantly higher crime rates compared to the rest of the dataset, possibly indicating urban areas with social issues or errors in data collection.

Outlier Details

Outliers in the CRIM column include neighborhoods with exceptionally high crime rates that may indicate severe social issues or possible data anomalies. These areas are often urban and may not represent the general trend across the Boston Housing dataset.
