Model-Specific Insights

  • Logistic Regression: Displays a well-distributed classification with balanced precision and recall. False positives are minimal, ensuring fewer unnecessary alerts for churn.
  • Decision Tree: Captures the majority of true positives but may overfit the training data, leading to slightly higher false positives.
  • Random Forest: Delivers the most accurate results with fewer false positives and negatives, owing to its ensemble approach.

Analysis Summary

The confusion matrices highlight the ability of the models to distinguish between churn and non-churn cases. The trade-offs between precision and recall are apparent in each model:

  • Random Forest: Ideal for datasets with complex patterns, offering consistent and robust predictions.
  • Logistic Regression: Effective as a baseline model, especially when interpretability is crucial.
  • Decision Tree: Provides clarity in decision-making but requires careful tuning to avoid overfitting.

Conclusion: Random Forest emerges as the top-performing model for churn prediction, balancing accuracy and generalizability.
