Loan Dataset Scatter Plots with Outliers

Post-Outlier Removal Summary

The removal of outliers has significantly improved the clarity of the dataset, allowing for better visualization of patterns and relationships between variables. The cleaned dataset now presents a more normalized distribution, helping to identify meaningful trends.

Key Insights Post-Outlier Removal

  • Applicant Income: After removing outliers, the distribution of applicant income appears more centralized, eliminating extreme high-income cases that previously distorted the analysis. The scatter plot now highlights the typical income range for loan applicants, making it easier to observe the relationship with loan approval.
  • Coapplicant Income: Cleaning the data removed anomalies such as zero or unrealistically low incomes, resulting in a more balanced dataset. A clearer correlation between coapplicant income and loan approval likelihood is now visible, with fewer cases skewing the results.
  • Loan Amount: The cleaned dataset has removed extremely high loan requests that were previously dominating the distribution. Post-outlier removal, loan amounts display a more normal and interpretable range, better reflecting typical loan request behaviors.

Improved Visual Insights

  • Applicant Income vs Loan Amount: The scatter plot now shows a smoother trend, where higher-income applicants generally request higher loan amounts. The absence of extreme data points emphasizes typical borrowing behavior.
  • Coapplicant Income vs Loan Amount: The cleaned dataset reveals a subtler relationship between coapplicant income and loan amount, particularly for applicants with coapplicants who contribute significantly to the household income.
  • Loan Amount Distribution: The histogram of loan amounts is more symmetrical, highlighting the common range for loan requests while reducing the influence of outliers.

Removing outliers has not only improved the quality of visualizations but also laid a stronger foundation for predictive modeling and data analysis. By focusing on the majority of the data points and reducing the noise caused by extreme values, the dataset now offers more reliable insights.
