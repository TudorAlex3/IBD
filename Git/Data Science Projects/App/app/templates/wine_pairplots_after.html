Wine Quality Dataset: Visualizations Post-Outlier Removal

Post-Outlier Removal Summary

After removing outliers, the wine quality dataset reveals more interpretable relationships between chemical properties and quality ratings. Outlier removal helps mitigate the influence of extreme data points that could skew trends and model performance. The visualizations now provide clearer insights into the typical patterns in wine quality.

Key Insights After Outlier Removal

  • Alcohol vs Quality: A stronger positive correlation is evident between alcohol content and wine quality. Removing outliers enhances the trend, showing that higher alcohol levels generally correspond to better wine quality ratings.
  • Chlorides vs Quality: Extreme chloride levels, which negatively impact quality, have been removed. This highlights the typical range of chloride concentrations associated with moderate-to-high-quality wines.
  • Density vs Quality: Removing outliers clarifies that high-density wines often score lower in quality, likely due to excess sugar or other non-fermented substances.

Improved Visual Insights

  • pH vs Quality: A subtle trend shows that wines with balanced pH levels (neither too acidic nor too basic) score higher in quality, which becomes clearer after removing outliers.
  • Sulphates vs Quality: Higher sulphate concentrations correlate with better quality, but this trend is more evident now without extreme sulphate values overshadowing typical patterns.
  • Volatile Acidity vs Quality: The negative impact of volatile acidity on quality is more pronounced post-outlier removal, emphasizing that wines with high volatile acidity generally receive lower quality ratings.

The cleaned dataset offers better insights for modeling and analysis. By focusing on the majority of observations, the visualizations align more closely with real-world trends, laying the groundwork for improved predictive accuracy and data-driven decisions.
