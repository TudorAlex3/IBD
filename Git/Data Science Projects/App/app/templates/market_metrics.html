Classification Report for Simple Models

The metrics for various models highlight their ability to handle different aspects of transaction data:

  • Simple Logistic Regression: Struggles to distinguish complex relationships, offering moderate accuracy and balanced class performance.
  • Decision Tree: Effectively captures patterns with reasonable accuracy and balanced performance across classes.
  • Random Forest: Excels at generalizing well across classes, with high precision, recall, and accuracy.

Insights on Model Performance

Each model demonstrates different strengths:

  • The Logistic Regression model shows a decent baseline performance, but lacks the complexity needed for nuanced predictions.
  • The Decision Tree balances simplicity with decent predictive power, handling varied datasets with reasonable success.
  • The Random Forest provides the best overall performance, showcasing high accuracy and robust class differentiation.

Conclusion

In summary:

  • Logistic Regression can serve as a foundation for simpler analyses.
  • Decision Trees provide a balanced approach with manageable complexity.
  • Random Forest stands out as the most reliable model for handling large, complex datasets, ensuring high accuracy and precision.
