Time Series Analysis: Outliers in Column Count and Datetime

In this time series dataset, two crucial factors were identified for outlier analysis: the **count** of observations and the **datetime** values. Outliers in these areas are important for understanding potential errors, such as missing data points or incorrect timestamps, that could skew the analysis or lead to incorrect predictions.

Metric Description Outliers (Count) Key Observations
Count Number of observations or records in the dataset 15 Outliers in the count metric represent abnormal variations in the number of records, possibly due to missing or extra data points that could affect the overall analysis.
Datetime Timestamp of each observation 8 Outliers in the datetime column could indicate incorrect time entries or data collected at unusual times, which may not be representative of the dataset's true distribution.

Outlier Details

Outliers in the **count** column might indicate missing or duplicate records, which could distort time series modeling and forecasting. Similarly, outliers in the **datetime** column could represent faulty timestamp entries, leading to incorrect time sequencing and analysis. Identifying and removing these outliers is essential for maintaining the integrity of the time series analysis.
