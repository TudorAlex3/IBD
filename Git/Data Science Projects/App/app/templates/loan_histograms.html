Histograms for Numerical Columns - Loan Prediction Dataset

Data Summary

Dataset Information: The Loan Prediction dataset consists of 614 records with 13 columns:

  • Loan_ID: Unique identifier for each loan application.
  • Gender: Gender of the applicant (Male/Female).
  • Married: Marital status of the applicant (Yes/No).
  • Dependents: Number of dependents of the applicant.
  • Education: Education level of the applicant (Graduate/Not Graduate).
  • Self_Employed: Whether the applicant is self-employed (Yes/No).
  • ApplicantIncome: The income of the applicant.
  • CoapplicantIncome: The income of the coapplicant (if any).
  • LoanAmount: The amount of the loan requested.
  • Loan_Amount_Term: The term of the loan in months (e.g., 360 months).
  • Credit_History: Whether the applicant has a credit history (1 = Yes, 0 = No).
  • Property_Area: The type of property area (Urban, Semiurban, Rural).
  • Loan_Status: The target variable indicating whether the loan was approved (Y) or denied (N).

Explanation of Histograms

The histograms show the distribution of the numerical features in the Loan Prediction dataset. For instance:

  • Applicant Income: Most applicants have a moderate income, with some outliers at the higher end of the scale.
  • Coapplicant Income: Many coapplicants have zero income, suggesting that many loans are taken by sole applicants.
  • Loan Amount: The loan amounts are typically between 100 and 200, with a few higher amounts.
  • Loan Term: The majority of loans have a term of 360 months (30 years).
