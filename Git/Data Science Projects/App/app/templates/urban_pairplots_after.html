Urban Sound Classification Pairplots After Removing Outliers

Post-Outlier Removal Summary

After removing outliers from the Urban Sound Classification dataset, the pairplots provide a more refined view of the relationships between the variables: **class**, **classID**, **fols**, and **salience**. The removal of extreme values helps reveal more typical patterns and improves the interpretability of these visualizations.

Key Insights Post-Outlier Removal

  • Class: Removing outliers has clarified the distribution of sound classes. The pairplot now reflects a more balanced and typical spread of sound categories, allowing for better identification of patterns within common sound types.
  • ClassID: With outliers removed, the distribution of ClassIDs shows a more normalized clustering. Extreme ClassID values that previously skewed the analysis have been filtered, enhancing the clarity of relationships.
  • Fols: Post-outlier removal, the distribution of frequency occurrences (fols) appears more consistent. Unusual peaks and troughs have been reduced, enabling more accurate observations of sound frequencies.
  • Salience: The pairplot for salience highlights a more typical range of prominence for sounds. Extreme salience values have been removed, making the visualization more representative of general data trends.

Improved Visual Insights

  • Class vs Salience: The scatter plot shows a smoother relationship between sound classes and salience levels, where typical trends can be more easily observed without the influence of outliers.
  • ClassID vs Fols: The cleaned dataset emphasizes more consistent and normal distributions of sound occurrences for various ClassIDs, eliminating rare and extreme values.

By removing outliers, the pairplots provide a more realistic representation of the underlying data. This refinement enhances the ability to draw meaningful conclusions and supports more accurate predictive modeling for urban sound classification.
