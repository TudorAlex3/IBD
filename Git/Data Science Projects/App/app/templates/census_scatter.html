Census Income Dataset Scatter Plots

Data Analysis Summary

This report provides insights based on scatter plots of the **Census Income Dataset (Adult CSV)**. The analysis examines relationships among numerical variables, highlights clustering, and identifies outliers.

Key Observations

  • Age: The age variable shows a strong clustering around the working-age group (20–50 years), with outliers at very high ages likely representing retirees or data errors.
  • Capital Gain and Loss: These variables are heavily skewed, with most values near zero. A small number of extreme values appear as outliers, possibly representing high-income individuals or reporting anomalies.
  • Education Number: Education levels show distinct clusters corresponding to standard educational milestones, such as high school (9–12 years) and college (16+ years). Outliers may represent individuals with exceptional education levels.
  • Final Weight (FNLWGT): This variable shows a wide distribution, with some very high values. These outliers may represent errors or rare demographic groups.
  • Hours per Week: Most individuals work between 30–50 hours per week. Outliers at both ends (very low or very high hours) may represent part-time workers, overworkers, or incorrect entries.

Outlier Analysis

  • Age: Outliers are individuals aged 85+ who may represent retirees or inaccuracies.
  • Capital Gain and Loss: Extreme values likely correspond to a small number of high-income earners with significant capital transactions.
  • Education Number: Outliers at the upper end may reflect individuals with unusually high educational attainment.
  • Final Weight: High FNLWGT values might represent demographic weighting anomalies.
  • Hours per Week: Outliers reflect workers with irregular hours, such as seasonal or self-employed individuals.
